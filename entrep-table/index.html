Entrepreneur Intrapreneur
Definition An entrepreneur can be defined as an individual that builds a new venture with relatively limited resources, solely bearing the risks and rewards of the venture. An intrapreneur is an individual who works on developing new ideas and products within the confines of the business that they already work at.
Objective To create something of socio-economic value To increase organisational competitiveness
Operations Externally, outside of an existing organisation Internally in the organisation
Driving motive
  • Innovations based on passion
  • Independence
  • Financial gains
  • Freedom to innovate
  • Company's backing
  • Company incentives
Innovation Both entrepreneurs and intrapreneurs NEED to innovate. They need to identify the market gap or niche and innovate accordingly. They need to thorogouhly research existing products/services, competitors, and patents/copyrights/trademarks.
Innovation justification Entrepreneurs and intrapreneurs need to develop prototypes and minimum viable product (MVP) to validate and support their innovation.
Mindset Problems are viewed as opportunities Problems are viewed as threats
Leadership support Support from several venture capital firms Support from their own company
Teamwork Entrepreneurs and intrapreneurs need to build competitive team capabilities. They need to bring a team together and define culture and behaviour. They define the roles and delegate work.
Parties served Customers The organisation & the entrepreneur themselves
Risk Vulnerable to all types of risk Limited risk
Decision-making Involves greater independence Involves greater collaboration
Market awareness The way entrepreneurs and intrapreneurs gain an understanding of the market is essentially the same. They must research and keep validating by interviewing people and getting insights to refine their products/services.
Funding Entrepreneurs and intrapreneurs need to pitch to external or internal investors respectively to secure support or funding for their idea. The pitching is very similar and competitive.
Timeframe Not time-bound Time frame stipulated by the organisation
